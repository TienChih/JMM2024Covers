What is a walk?

Deforming Walks

A spider-move of a walk \(v_0,\ldots,v_{k-1}, \color{red}{x},v_{k+1}, \ldots v_n\) replaces \(\color{red}{x}\) with \(\color{red}{y}\): \(v_0,\ldots,v_{k-1}, \color{red}{y},v_{k+1}, \ldots v_n\) so long as \(v_{k-1}\sim y\sim v_{k+1}\).

The Game

Two walks are equivalent \(\equiv\) if:

  • You can prune (anti-prune) from one walk to the other.
  • You can spider-move between the walks.
  • (The endpoints of the walk are fixed.)

Definition (C-Scull, 2022):
A graph morphism between graphs \(f:\widetilde{G}\to G\) is a homotopy covering map if given any \(v\) in \(G\) and \(\tilde{v}\in f^{-1}(v)\), walks of length 2 starting at \(v\) lift bijectively to walks of length 2 starting at \(\tilde{v}\) in a way that preserves endpoints.

Theorem (C-Scull, 2022): Let \() f: \widetilde{G}\to G\) be a homotopy covering map, and \(\phi, \psi:K\to G\) which are homotopic via \(H:K \times I_n \to G\). Suppose \(\phi\) lifts to a map \(\tilde{\phi}:K\to \widetilde{G}\) s.t. \(\phi=f \circ \widetilde{\phi}\). Then there is a lift \(\widetilde{H}:K \times I_n \to \widetilde{G} \) s.t. \(\tilde{H}|_{K \times \{0\}} = \widetilde{\phi} \) and \( f \circ \widetilde{H} = H\). In particular, \({\tilde{H}}|_{K \times \{n\}} = \widetilde{\psi} \) is a lift of \(\psi\) s.t. \( \widetilde{\phi} \simeq \widetilde{\psi} \).

Proposition (C-Scull, 2023): Let \(G\) be a connected graph and \(f:\tilde{G}\to G\) be a cover. Let \(\varphi:K\to G\) such that \(\varphi_*(\Pi(K))\subseteq f_*(\Pi(\tilde{G}))\), then \(\varphi\) lifts to \(\tilde{\varphi}:K\to \tilde{G}\) such that \(\varphi=f\circ\tilde{\varphi}\).
